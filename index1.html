The EYES function as sensory organs crucial for vision, capturing light and converting it into electrical signals that are interpreted by the brain to form visual images. They also play a role in non-verbal communication, expressing emotions and intentions through movements and expressions. Overall, the eyes are essential for perceiving the surrounding environment, gathering information, and experiencing visual stimuli.

The MOUTH serves as the primary organ for ingestion, speech, and communication. It facilitates the intake of food and liquids, initiating the process of digestion through chewing and saliva production. Additionally, the mouth enables vocalization and verbal communication through the manipulation of airflow and articulation of sounds produced by the vocal cords. Overall, the mouth plays a vital role in both nourishment and communication processes.
